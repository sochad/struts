Computer Science Department

Welcome to the Department of Computer Science. The department offers BS, MS, and PhD programs in Computer Science;
MS programs in Software Engineering, Information Systems, and Information Security and Assurance; and a BS program in Applied Computer Science with various concentrations.
The department also offers concentrations in the inter-disciplinary MS in Data Analytics Engineering and the PhD in Information Technology offered by the Volgenau School of Engineering.

Faculty in the department have research interests in networking, architecture, parallel and distributed computing, performance evaluation,
software engineering, multimedia, graphics and visualization, databases, software engineering, data mining, security, information systems, artificial intelligence, computer vision, and robotics.

  1. Masters of Science in Computer Science
      2. Master of Science in Computer Science is a breadth-based program. Students are required to complete 30 credits of graduate courses.
      Courses are grouped in the following five broad areas:
    1. Artificial Intelligence and Databases
    2. Programming Languages and Software Engineering
    3. Systems and Networks
    4. Theoretical Computer Science
    5. Visual Computing
  2. Masters of Science in Information Systems
      3. To provide a common background in the fundamentals of information systems, four core courses are required of all students.
      Six elective courses may be within an emphasis area or according to students' professional interests.
      Four core courses (12 credits) are required of all IS graduates:
    1. INFS 612 Principles and Practices of Communication Networks
    2. INFS 614 Database Management
    3. INFS 622 Information Systems Analysis and Design
    4. ISA 562 Information Security Theory and Practice
  3. Masters of Science in Software Engineering
      4. Four core courses are required of all MS-SWE graduates:
    1. SWE 619 Object-Oriented Software Specification and Construction
    2. SWE 621 Software Modeling and Architectural Design
    3. SWE 622 Distributed Software Engineering
    4. SWE 637 Software Testing
  4. Masters of Science in Information Security and Assurance
      5. To provide the necessary background and fundamentals of information systems security and assurance, the program has three courses that are required of all students:
    1. ISA 562 Information Security Theory and Practice
    2. ISA 656 Network Security
    3. INFS 612 Principles and Practice of Communication Networks OR CS 555 - Computer Communications and Networking
